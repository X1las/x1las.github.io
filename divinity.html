Divinity Mechanics

The 5e "Divinity" system is based on the action economy of Divinity Original Sin 2.

Premise

Combat resolves the same way as before, the only exception is that instead of the previous 1 action, 1 bonus action and 30ft of movement, the players get 4 action points to spend on whatever they please.

Cost Structure

Every normal choice a player can make in 5e is partitioned into action point cost as seen in the table below

Actions Bonus Actions Movement
2 action points 1 action point 1 action points per
half movement speed

Action points get refreshed after each turn, and have been assigned such that a player would be able to take there entire movement and do an action within one turn. This extends beyond the basic premise by allowing the player to make two attacks a turn if they are standing still, or take an additional bonus action to consume a healing potion if they so choose.

Additional Notes

  • By default, every type of bonus action can only be taken once per turn
    • You're not allowed to drink two potions a turn, but can stab someone with your offhand whilst drinking a potion.
  • A point of movement is equivalent to half your movement rounded down to the nearest 5 ft.
  • Additional points can be awarded or given depending on situation and gear
  • An inspiration can be chosen to be converted into an action point if needed
  • You can save two action points for another round by using the "Ready" action up to a max of 6
